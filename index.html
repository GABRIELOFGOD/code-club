‚ö° Power Supply Setup

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Multiple Power Sources Needed

You cannot power everything from Arduino's 5V pin or a single battery. Here's why:

  • Arduino 5V pin: Maximum 500mA (not enough for motors)
  • Motors: Can draw 1-3A each
  • Servo: Can draw up to 1A under load

üîã Recommended Power Setup:

Power Source 1: Arduino + Electronics

USB Power Bank (5V, 2A) or 9V Wall Adapter

Powers: Arduino, LCD, IR Receiver, Buzzer, small LEDs

Power Source 2: Motors & High-Power Components

6-12V DC Power Supply (2-3A)

Powers: DC Motor/Fan, Servo Motor, LED Strips

Power Source 3: AC Appliances (Optional)

Mains Power (110V/220V) through Relay

Controls: Lamps, Fans, Heaters (AC powered devices)

Power Distribution: USB/9V Adapter ‚Üí Arduino Uno ‚Üí 5V Rail ‚Üí LCD, IR, Buzzer ‚Üì GND Common ‚Üê‚Üí All Components Share Ground ‚Üë 6-12V Supply ‚Üí Motor Driver ‚Üí DC Motor ‚Üí Servo Motor (with capacitor for stability) ‚Üí LED Strips (with appropriate resistors) Relay Module: Arduino Pin 7 ‚Üí Relay Coil (5V from Arduino) AC Mains ‚Üí Relay Contacts ‚Üí AC Appliance

üö® SAFETY WARNINGS

  • AC Mains: NEVER work with AC power without proper knowledge. Get help from an electrician!
  • Relay Isolation: Always use proper enclosures for AC connections
  • Fuses: Add fuses to all power circuits
  • Ground Loop: Connect all grounds together, but use separate power supplies
